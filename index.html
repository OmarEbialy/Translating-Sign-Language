Project Description

The aim of this project is to implement an accessible, easy to use, inexpensive, and most importantly accurate sign language interpretation system that could be used by deaf/mute people to communicate with people who are not familiar with sign language, by converting the sign language gesture streams into either live text, or audible synthesized speech in real time.

  • Marketability and Demand our software product is categorized as accessibility software. Our software product provides an opportunity for said businesses to reach-out and communicate with their deaf/mute customers cheaply and save the cost of hiring sign language interpreters.

  • Other organizations and institutions could benefit from our software product as well. Airports, hospitals, schools, universities, and government institutions are all examples of organizations that occasionally deal with deaf/mute people, and thus could benefit from our software product.

  • Hospitals are perhaps some of the best examples of places where our software product could make an immense impact. The Americans with Disabilities Act mandates that U.S. hospitals receiving federal funding provide deaf patients with help to ensure effective communication (DoJ, 2016).
